# What you can ask me to do

You talk to me in plain language – there are no commands to memorise. Here's the range, grouped by task. Mix and combine freely ("find X, then fix Y").

**Project & progress**
- "What's the status of my Trados project?"
- "Show me the project statistics" – word counts, TM leverage, progress
- "How many segments are left to translate?"

**Find & read segments**
- "Show me the draft / unconfirmed segments"
- "Find segments containing <word>"
- "Go to segment 47"

**Translation memory & terminology**
- "Search my TM for <phrase>"
- "Look up the term <term>"
- "Which TMs and termbases is this project using?"

**Quality checks**
- "Run Trados verification"
- "Check the numbers / tags / terminology"
- "Find inconsistent translations of repeated source"

**Editing** – changes land as Draft so you can review them; nothing is saved until you save in Studio
- "Replace <X> with <Y> in the targets" – I preview first
- "Insert this translation into the active segment"
- "Add <source> = <target> to my termbase"
- "Add a comment on this segment"

**Batch tasks** – these run in the background; I'll tell you when they finish
- "Analyse files" – word counts & TM leverage
- "Pre-translate with my TM matches"
- "Update my main TM from the confirmed segments"
- "Generate / export the target file"

**Prompt library**
- "List my prompts"
- "Show me my Default Translation Prompt"
- "Improve this prompt and save it as a new one"

Tip: batch tasks and pre-translate run against the **last saved** state, so save in Studio first.
